Executive Summary

CylinderSeal is a decentralized payment network where every operator is an on/off-ramp. Users walk into a local shop with cash, walk out with digital money that works offline across a 5+ billion smartphone network.

The Insight: Stop building centralized infrastructure. Leverage existing retailers, NGOs, and individuals as liquidity nodes. Let market competition set exchange rates. Create jobs while building financial inclusion.

The Prize: $40B annual remittance market + $1.2T in everyday payments in developing world. First-mover advantage in decentralized mobile money.

The Solution: CylinderSeal

CylinderSeal Architecture: Devices + Super-Peers

Core Architecture

2-Tier System:

  • Tier 0: Devices — Personal transaction journals (offline-first)
  • Tier 1: Super-Peers — Validators + on/off-ramps (5 per region, Byzantine quorum)

Terminology Clarity:

  • Super-Peer: 1 of 5 regional validator nodes running CylinderSeal consensus (Rust backend, 5-node Byzantine quorum)
  • Operator / On-Off-Ramp: Local shop/NGO running the mobile app + holding cash reserves (hundreds per country). Each operator connects to the 5 super-peers in their region via gRPC.
  • Operators set their own spreads (2-3%), compete with other operators in the same town, users choose best rate
  • Super-peers validate operator transactions + resolve conflicts + enforce daily limits

Key Innovation: Every operator IS an on/off-ramp. No separate "central exchange" needed.

How It Works (3 Payment Channels)

Channel 1: App + NFC (Smartphone Users)

User A: "I'll pay you 100 OWC for groceries" (tap phones via NFC, no internet)
User B: Receives payment (added to personal journal)
Settles later via super-peer when online
Works anywhere. Offline. Instant.

Channel 2: WhatsApp (Smartphone Users in India, Brazil, Africa)

User: Sends money via WhatsApp message to contact
Message: "Send 500 INR to Raj" (encrypted in WhatsApp)
Operator confirms via WhatsApp Bot: "500 INR → 25 OWC ✓"
User's balance updates. Works offline. Syncs later.

Channel 3: SMS (Any Phone, Any Network, Even 2G)

User (on $5 feature phone): Texts "CS SEND 1000 PHONE:+923001234567"
SMS gateway verifies user balance with super-peer in real-time
Super-peer confirms: "User has 2000 PKR available"
System replies: "1000 PKR sent. Claim code: XY7Z9"
Receiver gets SMS: "You received 1000 PKR (code: XY7Z9). Visit operator to claim."
Both users' journals updated via super-peer sync when online
No smartphone needed. Works on 30-year-old technology.

Revenue Flow (All Channels)

Step 1: User walks in with 1,000 KES (cash)
Step 2: Operator scans QR (NFC, WhatsApp, or SMS code)
Step 3: User gets 980 OWC (operator keeps 2% = 20 KES)
Step 4: User spends 100 OWC, lends 200 OWC, saves 680 OWC
Step 5: After 50 transactions, user credit score: 78/100
Step 6: User borrows 5,000 KES at 0.5%/month via super-peer algorithm
Step 7: User invests in business, pays back, builds equity
Step 8: Operator earns $200/day from spreads. Super-peer earns 0.1% from operator spreads + loan origination fees.

Why This Works

Minimal Regulatory Burden (vs. Banks)

  • Operator doesn't need Safaricom/telecom carrier approval (M-Pesa model)
  • Operator doesn't need full bank license (just MSB or payment network registration in most countries)
  • Regulatory strategy: grassroots with NGOs (not positioning as bank or crypto)
  • 18-36 month regulatory window before competitors scale (FCA, RBI, CBN are still defining stablecoin rules)
  • Once registered: operators can run software, hold cash in regulated escrow, transactions settle peer-to-peer

Market Competition

  • Multiple operators → multiple rates
  • User chooses best rate (2% vs 8%)
  • Operators compete on trust, speed, convenience
  • No monopoly pricing

Self-Bootstrapping

  • Operators fund liquidity from cash spreads
  • Network effect: 10 operators > 1 operator
  • Early operators earn high margins (8%), attract competition, margins fall to 2%
  • Still much cheaper than Western Union (8%)

Truly Offline

  • Device-to-device payments work without internet
  • Super-peer sync happens when convenient
  • No dependence on telecom networks
  • Works on $10 Android phones from 2015

Cross-Country Remittances (One World Currency)

  • OWC Basket: 1 OWC = weighted average of USD, EUR, GBP, INR, ZAR, BRL (updated daily from live forex feeds)
  • Sender (Kenya): Walks in with 1,000 KES. Today's rate: 1 KES = 0.0077 OWC. Gets 7.70 OWC.
  • Sender sends to Pakistan: Sends 7.70 OWC to cousin's phone via WhatsApp or SMS
  • Receiver (Pakistan): Receives 7.70 OWC in journal. Today's rate: 1 OWC = 167 PKR. Can cash out for 1,285 PKR at any Pakistani operator.
  • Total cost: Just the operator spread (2% in Kenya, 2% in Pakistan = ~4% total, vs Western Union's 10%)
  • Mechanics: OWC is settled across countries by super-peer gossip (blockchain-style replication of signed transactions)

Business Model

Revenue Streams

1. Operator Spreads (Primary)

  • Operators set own cash ↔ OWC rates (market competition)
  • Typical spread: 2-3% (vs Western Union's 8%)
  • Platform takes 0.1% of operator spreads (0.002% of transaction volume)
  • $4.5T global retail payments × 0.2% blended fee = $9B/year at scale (platform takes 0.002% = $9M platform revenue)

2. Microloan Origination (Secondary)

  • Platform earns 1% of originated microloans
  • $200B originated/year × 1% = $2B potential (very high margin, 3% of TAM)
  • Algorithmic underwriting (no humans = cheap)

3. P2P Lending (Tertiary)

  • 10% platform fee on loan originated between users
  • Creates trust + verification layer
  • $5B/year volumes × 10% = $500M potential

4. Optional Integrations (Not Required)

  • KYC/AML services (only if regulated)
  • Rate feeds (only if not hardcoded)
  • No dependency on Flutterwave, Wise
